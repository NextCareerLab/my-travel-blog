# The Forgiveness: How EEG Data and Strategic Distance Healed My Family Trauma

## 1. The Invisible Wall: Why Paradise Feels Like Hell
People often move to Jeju Island seeking an instant cure for their broken souls. I was one of them. I stood atop **Gunsan Oreum**, watching the sunrise over the Pacific, yet my mind was a chaotic storm of past regrets. 

For years, I lived under the weight of being a "Good Child." My mother used this term to tame me, to keep me emotionally tethered to her own unhealed fragility. I thought I wanted to "save" her, but I eventually realized a painful truth: **You cannot save someone who does not want to save themselves.** 

To survive, I had to stop being her emotional savior and start being my own protector. This is the "Invisible Wall"—the gap between your physical freedom in paradise and your emotional enslavement to the past.

---

## 2. The Shadow of the Mother: Control Disguised as Love
In my journals, I found records from a decade ago showing I already knew my mother was trying to dominate and control me. She often said, "I only want what's best for you," but her subconscious truth was different: **She wanted her children to remain dependent infants forever.** 

This is what Carl Jung calls the **Shadow**—the repressed, inferior aspects of our personality. 
* **The "Good Mother" Persona**: On the surface, she was sacrificial and loving.
* **The Hidden Shadow**: Underneath, she harbored an insatiable need for attachment that delayed her children's independence and induced a sense of helplessness.
* **The Explosion**: My mother, who was used to suppressing all negative emotions, would occasionally explode in "madness-like" rage over trivial matters. This was her cumulative, unexpressed anger finally breaking through her "perfect" persona.

I realized that my anger toward her was a **Projection**. I was seeing my own unhealed wounds and my own dependency mirrored in her eyes.

## 3. Financial Chaos: The Biology of Self-Regulation
My lack of emotional independence manifested as a total lack of **Self-Regulation** in my finances. 

### The 50,000 Won Secret
Even as a teenager, I lacked a sense of money. When the IMF crisis hit Korea and my family lost everything, I didn't feel the pain. Why? Because I was secretly receiving 50,000 won (a huge sum then) every morning from an elderly neighbor. I spent it like water, never telling my family. I chose to live in a "fake abundance" rather than face the harsh reality of our poverty.

### The Credit Card Storm
This pattern followed me into my first job. I swiped credit cards to pay for drinks with people I didn't even like, just to avoid going home to face the "storm" within me. I was spending money I didn't have to fill a hole that couldn't be filled.

When I finally hit rock bottom with debt, the shame of losing my mother’s "trust" was unbearable. This stress triggered a physical collapse in her—panic attacks and vertigo. We were physically and emotionally destroying each other.

## 6. Healing Through Partnership: From Insomnia to Stability
Eight years ago, I met my partner. We are different: I used to run away after 3 months of "burning love," but he is a man of 7-year commitments. 

He didn't just give me love; he gave me **Regulation**. When my insomnia was at its peak, he stayed by my side all night, gently patting my shoulder until my body finally released its tension. For the first time, I felt safe enough to sleep deeply.
